


****** OVERVIEW OF THE CASE STUDY ******

. The Bright TV project analyzes viewership data from Jan - Mar 2016 to uncover audience demographics, content performance, and viewing behavior insights that can help improve programming and marketing strategies for subscriber growth.

******* DATA ANALYSIS AND INSIGHTS *******

. Data collection and cleaning - Raw viewership logs were imported into Databricks for initial processing and cleaning. Cleaned data was then exported to Google Sheets for analysis. Handled missing values in Gender, Province, and Race fields. . Analysis - Databricks SQL and Google Sheets aggregated views by day, time of day, channel, age group, gender, and province, driving metrics like daily viewership, session duration, and channel popularity. . Insights - key findings include peak viewing times on weekends and daylight hours, best performing channels like SuperSport and Trace TV, and audience concentration in Gauteng among Youth 20-30. . Recommendations - schedule premieres during peak Fri-Sun daytime slots, create short-form content under 15 minutes, prioritize Youth 20-30 demographics, and run geo-targeted acquisition campaigns in Gauteng and Western Cape.

****** SUMMARY AND INSIGHTS **********

. Built a Databricks SQL based analytics framework to process Bright TV viewership logs before analysis in Google Sheets.

. Generated detailed audience reports with age, gender, race, province, and time-of-day segmentation. 

. Identified programming and marketing opportunity to boost subscriber growth and engagement.
